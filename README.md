## 📈 Key Findings

### Finding 1

[The analysis of the happiest countries shows that European countries consistently rank at the top. This indicates that factors such as strong healthcare systems, economic stability, and social support contribute significantly to higher happiness levels.]

### Finding 2

[Countries with lower happiness scores are generally associated with lower GDP, weaker social support, and reduced life expectancy. This suggests that both economic and social conditions strongly affect national happiness.]

### Finding 3

[The scatter plot comparing GDP per capita and happiness score reveals a positive correlation between economic prosperity and happiness levels. Countries with stronger economies tend to report higher happiness scores.]

### Finding 4

[The heatmap analysis shows that social support and healthy life expectancy have strong positive correlations with happiness scores. This demonstrates that quality of life factors are equally important as economic growth.]

### Finding 5

[Regional analysis indicates that some regions consistently achieve higher average happiness scores than others. Differences in governance, healthcare quality, education, and living standards may explain these regional variations.]
